# Contributing to Healthcare Reform Ideas

Thank you for your interest in improving healthcare! This document outlines how to contribute.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Rule: Every Idea Must Have a Source WITH LINKS

**This repository does not accept unsourced ideas.** Every reform proposal must be attributed to:
- A specific person (e.g., "Mark Cuban", "Dr. Atul Gawande")
- An organization (e.g., "Kaiser Family Foundation")
- A published study or paper
- An official policy proposal
- Your own original idea (mark clearly as "Original proposal by [your name]")

**üîó LINKS ARE REQUIRED** ‚Äî Every source must include a URL so readers can verify the information.

**Why this matters:** Healthcare policy affects lives. Ideas need accountability, credibility, and verifiability.

## How to Contribute

### Option 1: Submit an Issue (Easiest)

Use our templates:
- **[New Reform Idea](../../issues/new?template=new_reform_idea.md)** ‚Äî Submit a new proposal
- **[Update Existing Idea](../../issues/new?template=update_idea.md)** ‚Äî Improve current content

### Option 2: Submit a Pull Request

1. Fork the repository
2. Create a branch: `git checkout -b add-idea-name`
3. Make your changes following the format below
4. Commit with clear message: "Add drug pricing idea from [Source]"
5. Push and open a PR

## Writing Guidelines

### Required Format for Ideas

```markdown
## [Idea Name]

**Proposed by:** [REQUIRED ‚Äî Name, organization, or "Original by Your Name"]

**Source:** [URL to interview, paper, policy doc, etc.]

**The Problem:**
[Specific description of the healthcare issue]

**The Solution:**
[Detailed explanation of the reform]

**Evidence:**
- [Study with link]
- [News article with link]
- [Real-world example with location/date and link]

**Challenges:**
- [Political obstacle]
- [Implementation difficulty]

**Implementation Path:**
- [ ] Step 1
- [ ] Step 2
```

### Source Quality Standards

| ‚úÖ Credible Sources | ‚ùå Avoid |
|---------------------|----------|
| Peer-reviewed journals (JAMA, NEJM, Health Affairs) | Random blogs or Medium posts |
| Government reports (HHS, CBO, FTC) | Unverified social media claims |
| Reputable news (Reuters, AP, WSJ, NYT, WaPo) | Partisan outlets without evidence |
| Expert testimony (Congressional hearings) | Anonymous or pseudonymous claims |
| Official policy documents | Conspiracy theories |
| Established think tanks (Brookings, AEI, Urban Institute) | Outlets with known misinformation |

### Writing Principles

- **Be specific** ‚Äî Include numbers, dates, and concrete mechanisms
- **Cite everything with links** ‚Äî Every statistic, quote, and claim needs a verifiable source URL
- **Consider trade-offs** ‚Äî No solution is perfect; acknowledge downsides
- **Stay constructive** ‚Äî Focus on solutions, not just complaints
- **Bipartisan tone** ‚Äî Frame ideas to appeal across political spectrum
- **International examples welcome** ‚Äî Other countries' systems are valid evidence

## Code of Conduct

- Be respectful and constructive
- No personal attacks on individuals or organizations
- Focus on policy, not politics
- Welcome diverse perspectives
- Back up disagreements with evidence and links
